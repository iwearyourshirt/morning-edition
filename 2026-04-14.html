Heads Up · Action Required
03 · Your Data

Backblaze stopped backing up your OneDrive and Dropbox.

Quietly, with no email announcement, Backblaze Personal Backup appears to have stopped including cloud-sync folders like OneDrive, Dropbox, iCloud, and Google Drive in its backups. Eight hundred upvotes and five hundred comments of furious users discovered this only when they went to restore something.

The theory: those folders contain “placeholder” files that aren’t actually on disk until opened, and Backblaze silently decided not to download them to back them up. The result is a silent data-loss hazard dressed up as a working backup.

Applies to you if……you use Backblaze + any cloud sync folder. Go check your exclusion list today. If you assumed it was covered, it almost certainly isn’t.
812 points · 499 commentsrareese.com

Forty percent of lost global calories come from beef.

33×
Calories of feed per calorie of beef

A new paper in an IOP journal runs the numbers and finds that cattle consume roughly 33 calories of feed for every 1 calorie of beef produced — and that beef alone accounts for 40% of all calories lost in the global food system. Not a moral argument; a thermodynamics one.
